Lab 4 - Pseudocoding and Problem-solving

Challenge

The goal was to write out a common task and the actions of a game in pseudocode.

Problems

Our biggest concern was coming up with what task and game to write our pseudocode for. Aside from that, we had no problems with the lab itself.

Results

Pseudocode for an Everyday Task

Changing an internal laptop battery:

  1. Turn off the laptop
  2. Set the laptop on a clean surface
  3. Flip over the laptop so that the bottom is exposed
  4. Remove the bottom cover of the laptop (this can be held in the form of screws/latches)
  5. Disconnect the battery’s power connector from the system board
  6. Remove the battery and replace it with a new model (the battery may also be held in by screws or latches)
  7. Put the bottom cover of the laptop back on

Pseudocode for a simple computer game

Connect 4

  1. Print a 6x7 slot board
  2. Accept user input for each column
  3. Validate user move (if they are able to commit the move)
  4. Move player's input piece to lowest empty space in the column
  5. Evaluate possible moves for computer
  6. Choose computer move
  7. Move computer's piece to lowest empty space in the column
  8. Confirm the winner when 4 pieces make a row, column, or diagonal
